Qeema Recipe

Qeema, a flavorful minced meat dish, is a beloved staple in South Asian cuisine. Traditionally made with ground beef, mutton, or chicken, it is cooked with a medley of aromatic spices, onions, tomatoes, and green chilies. The combination of cumin, coriander, garam masala, and turmeric infuses the meat with rich, warm flavors, making it a comforting and satisfying meal. Whether served with hot naan, paratha, or rice, Qeema offers a savory experience with every bite

One of the key elements that makes Qeema so delicious is its versatility. It can be enjoyed as a main dish, stuffed into breads, or even used as a filling for samosas or parathas. Some variations of Qeema include peas (matar) or potatoes, adding a delightful texture and contrast to the dish. The beauty of Qeema lies in its simplicity—quick to prepare yet packed with robust flavors that make it a family favorite for breakfast, lunch, or dinner.

Ingredients

  • Minced meat
  • Oil
  • Onion
  • Tomatoes
  • Garlic
  • Red Chilli poweder
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Once they start to crackle, add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  2. Add the ginger and garlic paste, and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the minced meat (qeema) and cook on medium heat, stirring frequently, until it changes color and no pink remains.
  4. Add red chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well, then add chopped tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes soften and form a thick gravy.
  5. Cover the pan and let the qeema cook on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, until the meat is fully cooked. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. If using peas or potatoes, add them at this stage and cook until tender.
  6. Add sliced green chilies and garam masala towards the end. Mix well and let it cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro and ginger slices. Serve hot with naan, chapati, or rice.
